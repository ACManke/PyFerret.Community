Hi Francisco,
Are your data on a regular grid?  Or are the lat, lon points scattered
in space?  If they are scattered then you can define a regular grid, read
in your data, and use one of the SCAT2GRID* functions to put them
on a regular grid.  With version 4.4, actually, the SCAT2GRID functions
are not available.  You will need to look at the script "objective" which
comes with the Ferret distribution.
yes? go/help objective                        ! and/or
yes? go objective_analysis_demo
If the lat, lon already define a regular grid, then you can either read them in
as in Ch2 Sec5.1.Reading ASCII files,  "Example 2—2 variables, 1 dimension"
(but you'll have 3 variables), and use lat and lon to define your axes, see
DEFINE AXIS/FROM_DATA, or just use your knowledge of the lat and lon
to define axes and read in the third variable only.
