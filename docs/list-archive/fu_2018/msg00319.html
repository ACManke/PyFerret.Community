Actually i want to calculate alongshore wind along sumatra  coast that makes an angle 135 with the horizontal plane.

^ As you are mentioning the 135 degree angle, we can do some calculation to convert your normal u (towards East) and v (towards North) winds to along/cross -shore winds by simplifying the coast to be straight line with your 135 degree angle. If such simplification is fine for you, then 

Along shore wind =    |v| * ( 1/sqrt(2) ) - |u| * ( 1/sqrt(2) )
Cross shore wind= - [ |v| * ( 1/sqrt(2) ) + |u| * ( 1/sqrt(2) )) ]         

   1/sqrt(2) = sin(45) or cos(45). |v| means magnitude of v.

In above formula, I have assumed 'towards North-West direction' as +ve along shore direction and 'towards South-West direction' as +ve offshore direction. Depending upon your convention you may apply -ve / +ve sign.

 This is according to my understandings, hope it helps.
