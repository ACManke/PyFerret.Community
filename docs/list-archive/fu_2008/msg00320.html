Hmmm. I read your message too quickly.
There IS a problem, but it is not with d/dy. MFC showed me the discussion of this point in a "really nice book" (Synoptic-dynamic meteorology in mid-latitudes. Bluestein, 1992). Bluestein notes that (in the northern hemisphere) a uniform west wind (or eastward current) is parallel to latitude circles, and therefore rotating (in this case dv/dx.gt.0). A uniform south wind (northward current) is converging and has du/dx.lt.0. However "since latitude circles are truly parallel to each other, there is no correction necessary for du/dy or dv/dy". He therefore finds the relative vorticity:
zeta = dv/dx - du/dy + (u/a)tan(phi) , where a is the radius of the earth and phi the latitude. 

The divergence is:

Div(u) = du/dx + dv/dy - (v/a)tan(phi)
And note that the correction terms will be very small except near the poles. 

Am I still missing something?

Billy
