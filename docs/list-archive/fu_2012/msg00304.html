Dear Kamoru Abiodun Lawal,

> I wish to regrid and at the same time want to maintain my K and L for the data below-------- 

>   let slon = xsequence(GLOBAL_LONGITUDE[i=28:63,j=11:40])  !...... Fit the domain here
>   let slat = xsequence(GLOBAL_LATITUDE[i=28:63,j=11:40])  
>   let PRECIP = xsequence(PR[i=28:63,j=11:40,k=1:1600,l=1:50])     !....... Change Variable to your desired variable here  

I suspect you shouldn't be using xsequence on PRECIP: it puts ALL data
on an x axis, where ferret probably expects precip(x,z,t) in this case.
You may be able to do it with the reshape() function.
You may have to make your slon and slat variables 3D as well:
something like

let mylon = slon + 0*z[gz=precip] + 0*t[gt=precip]

Let me know if you get this working, I would like to use it as well.
I use MATLAB myself for such cases: the griddata function is very
powerful.  It's a lot of work to write out netcdf data again, though.
