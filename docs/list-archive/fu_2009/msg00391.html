Dear All,

David, you are right: my variable was on a strangely defined time axis, thats why i didnt get the right values for @ave.

 TIME      TIME               361 i   16-DEC-1960 12:00    16-DEC-1990 12:00
 
       L     T                   TBOX      TBOXLO                TSTEP (days)
      16>  16-MAR-1962 12:00:00  30        01-MAR-1962 18:00:00    22719.5
      17>  16-APR-1962 00:00:00  30.5      31-MAR-1962 18:00:00    22750
      18>  16-MAY-1962 12:00:00  30.5      01-MAY-1962 06:00:00    22780.5
 
Then i defined the new time axis and also some new variables:

yes? sh axis/l=16:18 truemonth
 name       axis              # pts   start                end
 TRUEMONTHUJ TIME             361 i   16-DEC-1960 12:00    16-DEC-1990 12:00
T0 = 1-JAN-1900
   Axis span (to cell edges) = 10988
 
       L     T                   TBOX      TBOXLO                TSTEP (DAYS)
      16>  16-MAR-1962 12:00:00  31        01-MAR-1962 00:00:00    22719.5
      17>  16-APR-1962 00:00:00  30        01-APR-1962 00:00:00    22750
      18>  16-MAY-1962 12:00:00  31        01-MAY-1962 00:00:00    22780.5

yes? let newtemp=temp[gt=truemonthuj@asn] !i defined my var on this new axis

yes? let section = temp[l=16:18]
yes? let mean = section[l=@ave] !what Steve suggested

The mean and (original) temp with @ave is the same, and i want a weighted average. So i use the newtemp from now on. :)

yes? list/nohead temp[l=16:18@sum]/3,mean,temp[l=16:18@ave],newtemp[l=16:18@ave]
I / *:     9.851  9.894  9.894   9.821

Thanks All, Peter
