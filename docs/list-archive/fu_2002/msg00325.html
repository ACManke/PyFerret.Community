Antonio,
I think it would be difficult to have a standard ferret function to calculate wind stress curl because the choice of drag coefficient needs to be based on the research being done by an individual scientist. For our "Global" Upwelling Index calculation we use a non-linear drag coefficient based on Large and Pond 1981), where the draf coefficient is a function of wind speed. The formulation is written out at::

http://las.pfeg.noaa.gov/las/doc/wind_calc_details.html#CD

Our historical upwelling index, however, used a constant drag coefficient.

Once the drag coefficient is known, wind stress curl is easy to calculate in Ferret. Here's one way, using a centered derivative. Let u and v be the fields of wind components, rho is density, and Cd is drag coefficient:

let uvmag=(u^2 + v^2) ^.5
let taux=rho * Cd * u * uvmag
let tauy=rho * Cd * v * uvmag
let curl=tauy[x=@DDC] - taux[y=@DDC]
