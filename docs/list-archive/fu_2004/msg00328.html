Your i,j,k indices can be thought of as defining an x,y,z grid. So make that grid, then regrid to it, then write out the results. You could write out in some arbitrary x,y,z space (x=1:900, y=1:601,z=1:40) using the @ASN regridding transformation for instance. Or you could figure out what lat, lon, and depth correspond to your i,j,k grid, regrid to that, then write out the results in geographical, earth coordinates.
