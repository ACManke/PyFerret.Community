Hello All,
	Just a quick addendum to Ansley's note.  The @Pn change-of-pen,
while it is restricted to one character following the @P, does allow
hexadecimal characters to extend a ways past 9.  So you can use
@Pa (to get Pen 10), @Pb ...
	@Pg gives a nice thick blue label
But for "h" and beyond greyish labels are the result.
