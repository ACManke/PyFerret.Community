On Sun, Apr 28, 2019 at 10:23 PM William Kessler <william.s.kessler@xxxxxxxx> wrote:
Here are two old scripts that find individual harmonics algebraically:

Least -squares fit time series to C + Asin(wt) + Bcos(wt), where C is the mean, w is omega.

The first one (harm-6b.go) works for regular data with no blanks (then C is the simple time average).
The second (harm-blank.go) handles irregular time series with blanks and incomplete years (lots of ugly cross-terms, and the mean is part of the fit).

These do not plot anything, just set up variables. Output listing tells you how to use the defined variables.
