One idea to center the zero is to use levels (-inf)(your-other-levels)(inf)

A really simple blue-lightgray-red that works well for centered anomalies is

 0   20   20  100
45   95   95   95
55   95   95   95
100 100   20   20

(of course you can change the 95s to 100s to make the center white, but I find the light gray deemphasizes the near-zero values which I think is the goal here)

This is simple enough that you could have your script write it out as a dummy palette file every time, shifting the gray center to the zero of your data as determined before every SHADE/FILL.
